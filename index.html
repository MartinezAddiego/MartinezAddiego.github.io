ABOUT ME

Hello! Thanks for visiting my website. I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Georgia Tech supervised by Dr. Ratan Murty. I recently defended my dissertation as part of the Neuroscience and Cognitive Science programs at Georgetown University. There, I was co-mentored by Drs. Ella Striem-Amit and Maximilian Riesenhuber.

During my PhD I used fMRI and behavioral methods to study how the brain represents actions. Ultimately, we hope our findings may contribute to advancing brain-computer-interface (BCI) technologies. Down the line, I aim to incorporate computational models that may further complement cognitive frameworks of action representations in the brain.

I am passionate about open science and science communication, and I am always looking for opportunities to grow my skills. Please reach out if you think you have a project I may be interested in.

Feel free to explore this site to learn more about my work or get in touch!
